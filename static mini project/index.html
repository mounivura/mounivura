Crave DESSERTS

Dessert, the last course of a meal. In the United Kingdom dessert is likely to

consist of pastry, cake, ice cream, pudding, or fresh or cooked fruit. ...

In many cuisines, however, there is no usual sweet course; rather, fresh fruit, tea, or coffee constitute the end of the meal.

Their presence on the menu makes us feel satisfied after a meal, and compensates

for low blood sugar. The desire to improve your mood by ingesting sugar can

be a factor. Sweet snacks increase our production of the so-called hormone of happiness. Your habits also play an important role.

The word dessert is derived from the French word desservir, which translates to

“to clear the table.” This origin is apt, considering that the first use of

desserts was to wash down the aftertaste of a large meal with something sweet. ... However, the seventeenth century saw the advent of dessert cookbooks.
